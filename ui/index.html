01 / Method

Why most document checks fail

Visa rejections rarely stem from missing documents. They stem from logical inconsistencies across documents that automated systems miss and consultants overlook.

Rule 01
Adversarial interrogation

Every application is treated as if it needs to withstand an officer who is actively looking for inconsistency — not one who is rubber-stamping completeness.

Rule 02
Cross-document reasoning

The income on a payslip must match the deposit on a bank statement. The sponsor's declared income must cover program cost × 1.2. No agent sees documents in isolation.

Rule 03
Context-aware thresholds

A ¥2M minimum balance means nothing in GBP. Rules are resolved against the exact visa type, destination country, and applicant context — not hardcoded constants.

02 / Architecture

The structural encoding pipeline

A 30-page bank statement is 30,000 tokens. A 72B reasoning model has a 32k context window. The structural encoder converts raw documents to a typed semantic graph — 98% smaller, zero information loss.
